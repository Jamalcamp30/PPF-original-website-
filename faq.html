QUESTIONS

Answers
Before You Walk In.

The questions people actually ask before they commit — organized by who you are.

General

Who is PPF for?
Competitive athletes (ages 12–22), adults of any fitness level, and individuals with adaptive needs. Three paths, one coaching standard. You do not need to be in shape to start.
What ages do you train?
Athletes as young as 12. Adults of all ages. Integrated path members of all ages. There is no upper limit — only a coaching standard that adapts to where you are.
How does the 3-Day Experience work?
You sign up, come in for three coached sessions at no cost, and train alongside current members. Day 1 is about 60 minutes: assessment, orientation, and your first coached session. After the three days, you decide if PPF is the right fit — no pressure, no contract lock-in.
What should I bring to Day 1?
Athletic clothes, court or training shoes (no sandals), and a water bottle. We provide everything else. Arrive 10 minutes early so we can walk you through the space before your session starts.

Athletes

What age range do you train?
Athletes ages 12–22 — middle school through college. Programming is matched to training age and sport demands, not just calendar age. Younger athletes focus on movement quality and foundations; older athletes layer on periodized performance work.
Is training sport-specific?
Programming is built around the qualities your sport demands — speed, power, change of direction, strength, conditioning — not novelty drills. Coaches account for position, season, and competition calendar when writing your block.
How do in-season vs off-season blocks work?
Off-season emphasizes strength, power, and capacity. In-season shifts to maintenance, recovery, and availability — training supports performance on game day rather than competing with it. Your coach adjusts volume and intensity around practice and competition load.
Do you help with return-to-play after injury?
Yes. We coordinate with your physician or physical therapist and progress you through movement, load, and sport-specific demands. The goal is a clean, coached return — not a guessing game.
How are assessments run and progress measured?
Day 1 includes a movement and performance assessment. Benchmarks — sprint times, jump scores, lifts — are re-tested on a schedule so improvement is measured, not assumed. You see the numbers your coach sees.

Adults

I’m an adult and new to training. Is this beginner-friendly?
Yes. The Adult Performance path is built for all levels — including people who have never touched a barbell. Every session is coached, every movement is scaled to your ability, and your program progresses as you do. You will never be left to figure it out alone.
How often should I train?
Most athletes train 3–5 days per week. Adults typically train 2–4 days. Integrated members train 1–3 days depending on their program. Your coach will help you find the right cadence.
I have old injuries or joint issues. Can I still train?
In most cases, yes. Coaches scale movements and load around what your body can handle today, and build back capacity over time. If something is outside our scope, we’ll say so and point you in the right direction.
How does programming progress over time?
Blocks are periodized so volume, intensity, and movement complexity change with purpose. You’re not repeating the same session forever — and you’re not chasing random workouts. Progress is visible in your numbers and how you move.
Can I fit this around a full work schedule?
Yes. Most adult members train 2–4 sessions per week with early morning and evening options. We help you build a sustainable cadence rather than a heroic one that breaks down after three weeks.

Integrated Fitness

How is adaptive / integrated training handled?
Rebecca built the Integrated Path from the ground up, drawing on 18 years of special education experience. Sessions are individually designed around the member’s abilities, coached with the same rigor as every other path, and focused on building real independence over time.
What does the intake and assessment process look like?
We start with a conversation — Rebecca or an Integrated coach meets with the member and family to understand history, goals, and any medical or behavioral context. From there we run a low-pressure movement assessment and build a program that meets the member where they are.
What are the coach-to-member ratios?
Integrated sessions stay small. Most training is coached 1:1 or in very small groups so every rep is supported. If a member needs dedicated one-on-one coaching, that’s what they get.
What funding pathways are available?
Families use a mix of Medicaid waivers, private pay, and occasional scholarships depending on the situation. We’ll help you understand what fits — just ask and we’ll walk you through it.

Day Program

What does a typical day look like?
Participants arrive at 9 AM and leave at 3 PM. The day alternates coached fitness, life-skills lab, lunch and social time, a community outing or on-site project, and a closing reflection. Structure is consistent so participants know what to expect.
Is transportation provided?
Families are responsible for drop-off and pickup at our Cumming location. We have a clear, supervised arrival and departure routine. We’ll talk with you about logistics during intake so the first day runs smoothly.
How does enrollment work?
We run rolling enrollment with main openings in spring and summer. When we’re at capacity we keep a short waitlist. Start with a conversation — we’ll walk through fit, availability, and next steps.
What about Medicaid waivers and private pay?
Most families use a Medicaid waiver (such as NOW/COMP in Georgia) or private pay; some use a combination. We help families navigate the paperwork — just ask.

Memberships & Pricing

What membership plans do you offer?
Group coached sessions, private coaching, nutrition support, and member perks. Plans are matched to path and training frequency rather than a one-size-fits-all model. See the memberships page for current options.
Do I have to sign a long-term contract?
No long annual contracts. Memberships run month-to-month with clear terms. If PPF is the right fit, you stay because it’s working — not because you’re locked in.
Can I cancel or freeze my membership?
Yes. We support pauses for injury, travel, or life changes, and cancellation is straightforward. We’d rather you come back when the time is right than pay for sessions you can’t use.
Why is PPF priced the way it is?
Every session is coached — not self-guided floor time. That requires credentialed coaches, small groups, and real programming. The price reflects the standard we refuse to cut corners on.
