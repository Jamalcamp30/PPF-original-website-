The Program

A ONE-OF-A-KIND EXPERIENCE

PPF Integrated's Day Program offers the tools to live a life filled with valuable and engaging connections. We provide a program that assists families and individuals with the transition from secondary school into adulthood — as well as adults looking to further develop skills and quality of life.

Empowering and supporting adults with disabilities in developing skills for independence, health and fitness, and integration in their community based on self-directed interest is our sole focus. We make adulting fun and engaging for every participant within our facility and our community.

This side of the business is something truly extraordinary. Very few companies — very few businesses — do anything like this. The amount of impact within the community, within our participants' lives, and within their families' lives over the past 6 years is absolutely remarkable.

WHAT FAMILIES ASK US FIRST

The real questions — answered the way we'd want them answered if this were our family.

"I'm not sure if we'd fit here."
That's exactly the conversation we want to have. Bring your athlete in, meet the coach, see the room. No commitment, no pressure.

"Do your coaches actually have experience with this?"
Yes. Ask us who, for how long, and with which partners — we'll tell you by name. Our director, Rebecca Camp, holds an Ed.S in Special Education with an Autism concentration and has worked with individuals with special needs for 17+ years.

"What if they have a hard day?"
Hard days are part of training. Our coaches are trained to adjust — to regulate, to pause, to redirect. Progress doesn't require perfection.

"Can siblings train too?"
Absolutely. Integrated means the whole family has a place here — together or in parallel.

"We've been burned before."
We understand. Start with one visit. Meet the coach. Watch how your athlete is spoken to. That answers most of the real question.
