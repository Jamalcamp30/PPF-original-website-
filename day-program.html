THE PROGRAM

More Than
A Day Program.

The PPF Day Program is a structured, full-day experience for adults with disabilities who benefit from consistent programming, real coaching, and a community that knows them by name. We built it because what existed wasn’t enough.

It’s designed for adults transitioning out of secondary school, adults who need structured daytime programming, and families looking for a place that treats their person as a full adult — not a case file.

Rebecca Camp built and leads the program. Her 18+ years in education — specializing in special education — plus six years running the Day Program at PPF, shape every hour of the day. The rule that governs every PPF session applies here too: if it’s not coached, it doesn’t happen here.

Learn more about the coached foundation behind it on the Integrated Fitness page, or meet the team on the Founders page.

CAPACITY & ENROLLMENT

Small By
Design.

The Day Program is built to serve up to 50 participants at full capacity, with enrollment opening primarily in spring and summer windows. We run a small waitlist when we’re full so the experience stays personal and the staff-to-participant ratio stays right.

STAFF

Coached,
Not Babysat.

The Day Program is staffed by PPF coaches trained specifically for Integrated and Day Program work. We keep staff-to-participant ratios low so every person gets active coaching — not just supervision. The coached standard that applies to every other room at PPF applies here.

Rebecca Camp is on-site and directly involved every day — running intake, coaching, and culture. Learn more about her background and the rest of the leadership on the Founders page.

FUNDING

How Families
Pay For It.

MEDICAID WAIVERS

Many Georgia families fund the Day Program through a Medicaid waiver (NOW/COMP and similar). We work with families and support coordinators to align hours and billing correctly.

PRIVATE PAY

Families without a waiver can enroll through straightforward private pay — monthly with clear terms and no long lock-in. We’ll walk through pricing transparently during intake.

SCHOLARSHIPS

A limited number of partial scholarships are available each year for families in financial need. If funding is a barrier, tell us — we’d rather have the conversation than lose a good fit.

We help families navigate the paperwork — just ask.

FAQ

Day Program
Questions.

What are the daily schedule specifics?
Doors open at 9 AM and the program runs through 3 PM pickup. The day alternates coached fitness, life-skills lab, lunch and social time, a community outing or on-site project, and a closing reflection circle.
Is transportation provided?
Families are responsible for drop-off and pickup. We run a consistent, supervised arrival and departure routine every day. We’ll talk through logistics with you during intake.
How does enrollment work?
We run rolling enrollment with main openings in spring and summer. When we’re at capacity we keep a short waitlist. Start with a conversation — we’ll walk through fit, availability, and next steps.
What about funding — Medicaid waivers or private pay?
Most families use a Medicaid waiver (such as NOW/COMP), private pay, or a combination. A limited number of scholarships are available. We help families navigate the paperwork — just ask.
